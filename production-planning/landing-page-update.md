# Landing page of production next.js app update

We need to update the landing page of the prod app.
For this update we are adding a new section between the hero section and the section labeld "The Idea"
The purpose of this section is to give a quick onboarding style introduction to the app, so that the user understands what this app is about, what its functionality is, and also, at the same time, how he can get started.

This section will be a features section composed of several subsections that should keep the style of a standard popular landing page.

The section will not have its own heading, rather each subsection will have its own heading.

Please assume the role of a UI/UX expert, this section shouold look extremely appealing without making it look flashy, the desing should look professional, trendy, and sleek.

Please look at styling of the features section of landing pages of popular learning apps such as Remnote, Notion, etc for inspiration, but try to also match the styling to the general styling of the app, so we are not trying to clone but just match the standard of professionalism.

I will leave the actual sketch to your expertise, I will only share the content, your job is to make it look as appealing as possible without it looking flashy or in bad taste, as this is the one opportunity to engage potential users and convince them that this app is useful.

For each subsection I will share its heading and a couple bulletpoint style sentences, we dont have to use bullet points, however. Potentially we could use appropriate icons, or other elements to make it look more instructive.

In the case where we have 2 or more pictures per subsection, I sort of like Remnote's idea of changing the picture as the user hovers on a particular bulletpoint, so if a bullet point refers to one picture we display that picture, if it refers to another picture we display the other picture. Please take this point as a suggestion only, feel free to improve on this idea or try to find a better idea for the case where we have 2 or more pictures per subsection, there might be better design approaches.

Just for context I will mention some of the things I like about remnotes approach. they have a rich color schema for their features section, and each subsection seems to have its own subschema.

You can see a clear hiearchy of content, with headings, paragraph elements, bullet point style elements (with icons)

For the headings the highlight keywords with a different colors. The screenshots are wrapped in containers with a subtle design that makes the screenshot look much more professional.

From obsedian I like that they add a subtle border radius to the screenshots that match the border radius of their containers.

These are just some ideas, feel free to improve on them or come up with better ones. That being said here is the content
